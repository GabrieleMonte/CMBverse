Photon Optical Depth


The epoch of reionization is a period of time when the composition of the universe transitioned from being largely neutral to being ionized. During this time, the ultraviolet light emitted by the first stars reionized the neutral cosmic gas throughout the universe, resulting in the release of free electrons. This introduces a small probability of Thomson scattering for CMB photons, affecting the power amplitude. The amount of scattering is dependent on the optical depth at reionization, \(\tau\), which represents the opacity of free electrons to CMB radiation. We can quantify this effect by considering a photon from direction \( \hat{\pmb{n}}\) with temperature \( T(\hat{\pmb{n}})=\bar{T}[1+\Theta(\hat{\pmb{n}})] \), where \(\bar{T}\) refers to the mean temperature of the CMB and \(\Theta(\hat{n})\) represents the temperature anisotropy in that direction. This probability of the photon passing through unscattered is \(e^{-\tau}\), while the probability of the photon being scattered is \( 1 - e^{-\tau} \).From this, the temperature observed should follow:

\( T(\hat{\pmb{n}})=\bar{T}[1+\Theta(\hat{\pmb{n}})e^{-\tau}] \)

Thus, the effects of any anisotropies are uniformly reduced by \(e^{-\tau}\). This effect only occurs at smaller angular scales (higher multipoles), since at the time of reionization, the horizon size -- the distance at which causal connections can occur -- was much smaller and thus limited the angular size that could be affected.
