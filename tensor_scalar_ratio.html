Tensor to Scalar Ratio


The tensor-to-scalar ratio \(r\) represents the ratio of tensor perturbations to scalar perturbations in the CMB. Scalar perturbations are due to the density fluctuations in the early universe on the surface of last-scattering, and these polarized waves are labeled as E-modes. Tensor perturbations, on the other hand, are swirling perturbations that create a unique polarization geometry called B-modes. Thus, as we increase the tensor-to-scalar ratio, we expect to see the power amplitude of these B-modes to increase, deviating from the general power-law shape on our BB polarization spectrum.

The tensorial nature of these B-modes can only be sourced by gravitational perturbations, which would have permeated the observable universe at the time of last scattering. Since these primordial gravitational waves had wavelengths on the order of the size of the observable universe at that time (high angular scales, low multipoles), we expect the amplitude increase to manifest at these scales as well.

The existence of primordial gravity waves is a key signature of inflationary cosmology models; thus, the detection of B-mode polarization would be significant evidence for cosmic inflation.
