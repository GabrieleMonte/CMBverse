Neutrinos


Neutrinos, unlike photons, were free-streaming in the early universe. This free-streaming leads to a different distribution of energy and can damp small-scale anisotropies. A larger presence of neutrinos also contributes to the radiation density of the early universe, contributing to its expansion and effect the damping of the CMB we measure today.
