Optical Depth and Spectral Amplitude


Optical depth at reionization \(\tau\) and the spectral amplitude \(A_s\) act degenerately. As described in our Photon Optical Depth page, the effects of reionization uniformly reduce the power spectrum \(C^l\) by a factor of \(e^{-2\tau}\). However, \(C^l\) is also uniformly proportional to the scalar amplitude, \(A_s\); thus, observation only constrains the combined value of \(A_se^{-2\tau}\). Utilizing the Plank 2018 data set, this value is calculated to be about \(1.88\times10^{-9}\).

This degeneracy can be broken by examining how an increase in \(\tau\) results in an increase in power at low multiples. This is due to the unique reionization effect associated with the optical depth which occurs at large angular scales (lower multipoles) representing the size of the horizon at reionization. For more information, visit the Photon Optical Depth page.
