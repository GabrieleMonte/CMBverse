Primordial Power Spectrum

The Primordial Power Spectrum (PPS) measures the curvature of perturbations in the CMB. The formula for the PPS is \( \Delta^2_{\mathcal{R}} (k) = A_s \left( \frac{k}{k_\star}\right )^{n_s-1} \). For these models, everything in this equation is kept constant except for \( A_s \), the amplitude, and \( n_s \), the spectral index. As seen in the equation, if ns increases the spectrum will tilt upwards as more power will be given to small scales (larger multiples). On the other hand, if As of the PPS increases, the amplitude of the CMB will increase as well.
