Dark Matter


Dark matter in the early universe explains the pattern of anisotropies in the Cosmic Microwave Background, as photons would be deflected by the dark matter and create hot and cold spots on the CMB. The dark matter density also affects the timing of the transition from a radiation-dominated universe to a matter-dominated universe, therefore influencing the heights and positions of the peaks in the power spectrum.


We can initially just change the amount of dark matter in the universe \(\omega_{cdm}\). By holding the amount of baryonic matter \(\omega_{b}\) constant, we then expect the total matter content to change accordingly.
