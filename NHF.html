Neutrino and Helium Fractions


The number of relativistic neutrinos species \(N_{\nu}\) and the Helium fraction \(Y_{\text{He}}\) have degenerate damping effects on the CMB power spectra. Increasing \(N_\nu\) increases the radiation energy density, slowing down the expansion of the universe and thus lengthening the radiation-dominated stage of the universe. This effectively increases the rate of scattering that occurs, enhancing the damping effect on the power spectra. Meanwhile, increasing the Helium fraction ultimately increases the amount of free electrons, leading to increased damping effects due to scattering. We can mathematically describe the relationship between \(N_\nu\) (via its effect on the expansion rate \(H\)) and the Helium fraction (via its effect on the free electron number \(n_e\)) by the equation:

\( \theta_d=(H/n_e)^{1/2}\theta_s

For a fixed \(\theta_s\), there is a clear degeneracy on the damping angle \(\theta_d\).
