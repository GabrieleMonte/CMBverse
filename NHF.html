Neutrino and Helium Fractions


The neutrino number \(\N_{\nu}\) and the Helium fraction \(Y_{\text{He}}\) act degenerately. Increasing the amount of Neutrinos in the early universe lengthens the radiation-dominated stage of the universe, thus enhancing the damping effect on the power spectrum at higher multipoles. Meanwhile, increasing the Helium fraction serves to increase the amount of free electrons, leading to increased damping effects due to scattering.
