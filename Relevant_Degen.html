Relevant Degeneracies


Optical Depth and Spectral Amplitude

Optical depth at reionization \(\tau\) and the spectral amplitude \(A_s\) act degenerately. As described in our Photon Optical Depth page, the effects of reionization uniformly reduce the power spectrum \(C^l\) by a factor of \(e^{-2\tau}\). However, \(C^l\) is also uniformly proportional to the scalar amplitude, \(A_s\); thus, observation only constrains the combined value of \(A_se^{-2\tau}\). Utilizing the Plank 2018 data set, this value is calculated to be about \(2.7\times10^9\). This degeneracy can be broken by examining the effects of polarization due to reionization, which give an independent measure of reionization effects. For more information, visit the Photon Optical Depth page.
